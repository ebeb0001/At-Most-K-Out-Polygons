Project authors

This project was created as part of an academic exploration of at most k-out polygons , combining theoritical analysis with a full interactive implementation.

  • Ethan Bebelambou - Implementation
  • Ziyad Haltout Rhouni - Theoritical analysis

Work distribution

The work on this project was divided between the theoritical foundation and the development of the interactive enumerator:

  • Ethan Bebelambou implemented the enumeration algorithm, point-set editor, polygon operations and all visualization components
  • Ziyad Haltout Rhouni focused on the theoritical part of the project: understanding the definitions, analyzing the enumeration algorithm, and verifying the geometric conditions such as insertability, digability, and embeddability in the implementation.

Notes on implementation

The implementation presented on this webpage is faithful to the algorithm described in the paper, but with two important differences:

  • We did not perform the preprocessing steps suggested in the original paper.
  • One of the condition in the loops of the enumeration algorithm (q ≺ pi) made it impossible to reach all at most k-out polygons in practice. After investigation, we removed that condition, and this resolved the issue. As a result, our implementation is slightly from the exact formal version described in the paper, but produces the complete family of polygons as expected.

These differences do not changes the conceptual nature of the algorithm, bu they modify the enumeration behavior enough to be worth highlighting.
